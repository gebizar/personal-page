About Me

Hello!

As a recent graduate of the University of Washington Information School, I strive to leverage my broad skillset to shape the ever-changing landscape of the tech-world. Having been raised in Singapore, Taiwan, and the U.S., my diverse upbringing has strengthened my need to be inclusionary, and therefore bolster diversity in any community I belong to. My want to bridge communities is further enforced, by my two-years as an resident adviser in which I utilized Mandarin to engage with International Students and eventually incorporate them as part of the larger UW community. In addition to being in student leadership, having participated in many project teams through school and my internship in Intel has given me insight to aligning project visions between those of engineers, project managers, and data scientists. My experience in having spent hours tirelessly coding and enthusiastically engaging with those that I've presented projects to (regardless of language) has culminated in my ability to bring visions to life on all fronts.

For further information, please see my resume

Experience

Intel; Nonvolatile Solutions Group (NSG)

Having interned at Intel has definitely solidified my want and need to be within tech. The full experience of soaking in a beautiful city such as Folsom, CA. working alongside talented individuals, and being under the tutelage of motivated mentors was an immense privilege to me. I was able to learn create websites and understand the logistics behind internal products used by tech giants such as Intel, as well as develop a grasp on the inner machinations of the tech industry as a whole. Although my skills as a web developer vastly improved over the course of this internship it has also opened my eyes to the number of possibilites in tech and has stirred in me an excitement to meet other likeminded individuals.

For more information feel free to email me with inquiries regarding this internship. I'd also be more than happy to consult anyone who is seeking an internship such as this one.

My supervisor during my time at Intel was Kathryn Carruthers, an absolute cornerstone in Intel and instrumental to how our team functioned. For more questions on her please refer to LinkedIn or feel free to email me.

Resident Adviser

Over the course of two years spent as a Resident Adviser, I think I've fully bolstered the skills of conflict mediation and bridging communities. In particular, I spent time in the dorms Mercer Court and Lander, both of which are specifically catered towards international students due to their availability over the Winter Break. My ability to speak Chinese has garnered testimonials in my ability to bridge the culture gap, ease tension, and build rapport with people, both domestic and international. In 2017, I achieved the "Dubs Up!" award, an granted to communtiy leaders at UW, of which only two were granted to our building staff of over 40. My love for interaction with all walks of life and strong social skills lead me to believe that I'm a strong candidate for any position that interacts with a diverse cast of occupations, origins, and creeds. From engineers to non-technical folks to the international and domestic, my life's mission is bridge communities and challenge people to achieve their best.

For more information regarding my time as an RA please feel free to contact me or my supervisor's Jeffrey Perkins or Amanda Roy via email or LinkedIn

This Website!

This website was developed as a testament to my abilities as a front-end developer and a more expansive, holsitic view of my resume. I initially wanted to create this website using a framework such as Angular or React, but recently read an article about the over-abundance of single-page websites that utilize frameworks (unncessary overhead;) or websites that needlessly use flowery, multi-page html that serve to detract from user experience. Everything that you see was handcoded, without the help of templates and was storyboarded prior to development for flow and ease.
